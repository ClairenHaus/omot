Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025  |  Owning My Own Truth, Inc.

A note about your safety. We understand that visiting this website may carry personal risk for some users. We have designed this site with survivor safety in mind. Use the red "Exit Site Quickly" button at the top of any page to leave immediately. You can also clear your browser history after your visit by pressing Ctrl+Shift+Delete (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+Delete (Mac).

1. Who We Are

Owning My Own Truth, Inc. ("OMOT," "we," "our," or "us") is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (EIN: 82-4604330) serving survivors of domestic violence in Northern Bay Area, CA and Metro Atlanta, GA. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and protect information submitted through our website at https://owningmyowntruth.org and related services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information you voluntarily provide to us, including:

  • Name (first name is sufficient — last name is optional)
  • Contact information (phone number or email address, based on your preference)
  • Geographic region (Northern Bay Area, CA or Metro Atlanta, GA)
  • Type of support requested
  • Any additional information you choose to share in a message or form field

We also collect limited technical information automatically, including your IP address, browser type, and pages visited on our site. This information is used solely for site security and basic analytics.

3. How We Use Your Information

Information you submit is used only to:

  • Respond to your request for support, information, or volunteer opportunities
  • Connect you with services and resources relevant to your needs
  • Send you communications you have requested (such as workshop updates or our newsletter)
  • Process and acknowledge donations
  • Improve our programs and services on an aggregated, anonymized basis

We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Confidentiality of Survivor Information

We take the confidentiality of survivors' information with the utmost seriousness. Information shared through our intake form or by contacting OMOT directly is:

  • Accessible only to OMOT staff and volunteers directly involved in your care
  • Never shared with third parties without your explicit, informed consent — except as required by law (see Section 7)
  • Stored securely and handled with strict internal protocols
  • Never used to identify or locate you without your permission

If you are concerned about the safety of your information, please contact us directly at info@owningmyowntruth.org or [PHONE NUMBER].

5. Third-Party Services

Our website uses the following third-party services, each with their own privacy policies:

  • HubSpot — We use HubSpot to process intake forms, volunteer applications, and email subscriptions. HubSpot's privacy policy is available at legal.hubspot.com/privacy-policy.
  • Donation processor — Donations are processed through a secure third-party payment processor. We do not store payment card information on our servers.
  • Google Analytics — We may use aggregated, anonymized analytics to understand how our website is used. No personally identifiable information is included in analytics data.

6. Cookies

Our website uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to maintain basic site functionality and analytics. You may disable cookies in your browser settings, though some site features may not function as intended. We do not use cookies to track individual user behavior for advertising purposes.

If browser history or cookies are a safety concern for you, we recommend using a private or incognito browsing window when visiting this site.

7. Legal Disclosure

We may disclose your information when required by law, including in response to a valid court order, subpoena, or legal process. We will make reasonable efforts to notify you before disclosing your information if legally permitted to do so.

Please note that mandatory reporting laws may apply in certain situations involving minors or imminent harm. Our staff is trained on applicable reporting requirements.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy or as required by law. You may request deletion of your information at any time by contacting us at info@owningmyowntruth.org.

9. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Opt out of email communications at any time using the unsubscribe link in any email we send
  • Contact us with any concerns about how your information is handled

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@owningmyowntruth.org.

10. Children's Privacy

Our website is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has submitted information to us, please contact us immediately and we will delete it.

OMOT does serve families with children through our programs. In these cases, information is collected and handled in accordance with all applicable laws regarding the privacy of minors.

11. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the personal information we collect. Our website uses HTTPS encryption. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We encourage you to use a private browsing window and to clear your browser history if you are concerned about others accessing information about your visit.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our website after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
