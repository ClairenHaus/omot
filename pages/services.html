Service 01

Community Outreach

Healing happens in community. Our outreach work connects survivors with trusted organizations, emergency resources, and networks of support — so no one faces their situation alone.

OMOT builds and maintains relationships with shelters, legal advocates, healthcare providers, and community organizations across Northern Bay Area, CA and Metro Atlanta, GA. When you reach out, we do not just hand you a list of phone numbers — we make connections on your behalf, walk alongside you through referrals, and follow up to make sure you are getting what you need.

Resource navigation

We help you find the right services — shelter, food, legal help, childcare — and cut through the confusion of where to start.

Warm referrals

We make introductions, not just referrals. You go into every connection knowing someone is already vouching for you.

Follow-up support

We check in. If something is not working, we find another path with you.

Community partnerships

OMOT works with trusted local organizations in both regions so survivors have access to a full network of care.

Service 02

The OMOT Program

Our flagship program is a holistic, individualized support system that addresses the full range of what survivors need — not just safety, but stability, autonomy, and healing.

We recognize that leaving an abusive situation is not just an emotional decision — it is a logistical, financial, and legal one. The OMOT Program provides hands-on, wraparound support to help survivors address all of those dimensions at once, at their own pace.

🚗

Transportation

Getting to appointments, court dates, or safe locations should not be a barrier. We assist with transportation logistics for program participants.

🧠

Counseling

Individual and family counseling support to help survivors process trauma, rebuild self-worth, and develop healthy relationship patterns.

⚖️

Legal Advocacy

Guidance through protective orders, custody situations, and legal processes — with connections to attorneys and legal aid organizations.

💰

Financial Literacy

Building the financial knowledge and skills to establish independence — from opening accounts to understanding credit and budgeting.

📋

Life Management Skills

Practical skills and support for rebuilding day-to-day life — from employment readiness to parenting resources and self-advocacy.

🏠

Housing Referrals

Connections to emergency shelter, transitional housing, and long-term housing resources in both service regions.

Who can access the OMOT Program?

The OMOT Program is available to survivors of domestic violence, emotional abuse, and related trauma in the Northern Bay Area, CA and Metro Atlanta, GA service areas. You do not need to be in immediate crisis to access support — you just need to reach out.

There are no fees for program participation. OMOT is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization.

Service 03

Workshops

Knowledge is protection. Our monthly workshops give survivors — and those who love them — the tools to recognize abuse, understand their options, and build a plan to move forward safely.

OMOT hosts workshops across both service regions, creating safe, confidential spaces where survivors can learn, connect, and be witnessed. Topics are practical, trauma-informed, and led by people who understand what you are navigating from the inside.

Workshop topics include:

✓ Understanding domestic abuse — types, patterns, and cycles
✓ Recognizing warning signs and red flags
✓ Building a personalized safety plan
✓ Rebuilding self-esteem and identity after abuse
✓ Healthy relationships and boundary setting
✓ Navigating the legal system as a survivor
✓ Parenting through and after abuse
✓ Financial independence and rebuilding

Workshops are held monthly in both regions. Space is limited to maintain a safe and intimate environment.
