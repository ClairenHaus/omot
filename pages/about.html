Our story

Born from survival.
Built for community.

Owning My Own Truth was founded on the belief that no one should have to suffer in silence. We exist to walk alongside survivors — from the first moment of courage through every step toward healing and independence.

Our Mission

To break the inter-generational cycle of abuse by empowering survivors to move from victimization to emotional health, independence, and community — through counseling, advocacy, education, and holistic support.

Our Vision

A world where every survivor has access to the resources, community, and truth-telling they need to reclaim their identity, safety, and future.

Who We Serve

Men, women, and children experiencing physical, mental, and emotional abuse in underserved communities. We serve survivors in Northern Bay Area, CA and Metro Atlanta, GA.

Our founder

Tina Robinson

CEO & Founder

Owning My Own Truth was born from a place most people never want to go — and from a woman who chose to turn her pain into purpose.

Tina Robinson is a mother, a Master Hairstylist, a FEMA-trained Crisis Counselor, and a survivor. After living through her own experience of abuse, she made a decision that would change not just her life, but the lives of countless others: she would speak. She would reach back. She would build something that did not exist when she needed it most.

Founded alongside co-founder Jeania Robinson, OMOT was established to fill a gap in underserved communities — where survivors often face abuse in silence, without access to culturally sensitive support, legal resources, or a community that truly understands their experience.

Tina brings her FEMA crisis counseling training, her outreach experience, and the credibility of lived truth to every interaction. She does not just talk about healing — she is proof of it.

"I no longer live as a hostage to fear or judgment from others. Today, I speak and live from a place of victory. My life's mission is to break the generational pattern of people suffering in silence."

— Tina Robinson, CEO & Founder

What we stand for

Our core values

Compassion

Every survivor is met exactly where they are — without judgment, without pressure, and with complete respect for their timing and choices.

Confidentiality

Your safety and privacy are non-negotiable. What you share with OMOT stays with OMOT. We never share information without explicit consent.

Empowerment

We do not rescue — we walk alongside. Our goal is always to help survivors build the knowledge, tools, and confidence to lead their own lives forward.

Community

Healing does not happen alone. We build connection — between survivors, advocates, partners, and the broader community — because belonging is part of recovery.

Truth

Our name says it all. Owning your own truth is an act of courage. We hold space for the real, unedited stories of those we serve — and honor the power in speaking them.

Sustainability

We are here for the long haul. Our programs are designed to create lasting change — not just immediate relief — so survivors can build lives that hold.

"The truth will set you free."
